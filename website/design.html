Anxiety In Lines
Game Design Game World Development

Design

Everyday queue anxiety as mechanics

The design translates the ordinary experience of waiting in line into rules, data, and decisions that create uncertainty and social pressure.

Original Life Experience

Waiting in line becomes tense when the pace is unpredictable, people ahead delay progress, and the player has little control in a confined social space.

Core Emotion

Anxiety created by uncertainty, waiting, limited agency, and pressure from nearby strangers.

Core Mechanic

Manage position in a queue under a time limit. Move forward, negotiate, hold position, choose a line, or let others go first.

NoP

Number of people ahead of the player

RWT

Random waiting time for each NPC

GT

Remaining in-game time

Outcome

Win if NoP reaches zero before GT expires

Framework Graph

Queue system structure

The graph maps the original life experience into game world conditions, player input, resources, and win/loss states.

Player Decisions

Queue pressure becomes choice pressure

Move forward

The player advances when the next position opens. This expresses the desire to reduce waiting and regain control.

Negotiate

The player can attempt to persuade or cut in, but failure costs time and can increase tension.

Wait or yield

Waiting preserves social order but may weaken the player's chance to reach the counter before time expires.
